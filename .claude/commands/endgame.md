# Endgame Planning

Delegate to `strategist` subagent.

Read `.claude/skills/case-strategy/SKILL.md`.

Workflow:

1. Define the endgame with the user:
   - What does success look like? (Specific, concrete, dated.)
   - What does failure look like?
   - What's an acceptable middle outcome?
   - What are the absolute non-negotiables?

2. Work backward:
   - For success: what must happen 1 month before? 3 months? 6 months? a year?
   - What are the gating items?
   - What's the critical path (sequence that cannot be parallelized)?
   - Where are the dependencies?
   - Where are the risks?

3. Identify decision points:
   - Forks where strategy must commit (settlement vs. trial; appeal vs. accept; criminal plea vs. fight)
   - When each fork must be decided
   - What information is needed to decide each fork

4. Build Plan B / C / D:
   - If endgame A fails by [date], what's Plan B?
   - What decisions preserve optionality?
   - What decisions foreclose options?

5. Budget:
   - Time required
   - Money required
   - Attention required
   - Health / energy required
   - Can the user sustain this?

6. Output:
   - One-page endgame summary
   - Critical path with milestones
   - Decision points with deadlines
   - Plan A, B, C
   - Resource budget
   - Recommendations for consultations (attorney on [...]; therapist on sustainability)

7. Save to `08-research/endgame-<YYYYMMDD>.md`.

8. **GUARDRAIL**: Present options. Do not recommend. The user decides.

Log in `_session-log.md`.

The endgame answers: what are we doing this for, and how do we get there? Without it, every filing is reactive. With it, every filing serves a purpose.
