# Court Filing Format

Filing format failures get pleadings rejected, returned, or weighed against the filer. This skill encodes the format requirements for the most common CPS jurisdictions plus federal courts. Verify current local rules — they change.

## Universal defaults (when in doubt)

- Times New Roman 12pt
- Double-spaced body; single-spaced headings, captions, block quotes, footnotes
- 1-inch margins
- Pages numbered (bottom center or bottom right per local rule)
- Each filed document with: caption, body, signature block, certificate of service
- Proposed orders filed separately or attached per local rule

## Wisconsin Circuit Court

### Caption (CHIPS / juvenile court)

```
STATE OF WISCONSIN          CIRCUIT COURT          [COUNTY] COUNTY
                              JUVENILE DIVISION

IN THE INTEREST OF:                        )
                                           )      Case No. [YYYYJC######]
[CHILD INITIALS], a child under              )
the age of 18,                             )      [DOCUMENT TITLE]
                                           )
                                           )
```

### Other Wisconsin specifics

- Appellate brief font / format: Wis. Stat. §809.19; Times New Roman 12pt, certain page/word limits
- Wisconsin Court of Appeals brief: §809.19(8) — specific certification language required
- Confidentiality: ch. 48 mandates confidentiality; CHIPS proceedings are not public; case file restricted
- E-filing: efile.wicourts.gov; mandatory for attorneys, optional for pro se
- Wis. Stat. §806.04 (declaratory judgment) — for pre-litigation declaratory relief
- Service: Wis. Stat. §801.11

## Minnesota District Court

### Caption (CHIPS / juvenile court)

```
STATE OF MINNESOTA          DISTRICT COURT
COUNTY OF [COUNTY]          [JUDICIAL DISTRICT] JUDICIAL DISTRICT
                              JUVENILE COURT DIVISION

In the Matter of the Welfare of:
[CHILD INITIALS],                    Court File No. [##-JV-##-####]
Child.
```

### Other Minnesota specifics

- Minn. Stat. §260C governs CHIPS
- Minn. R. Juv. Prot. P. govern procedure
- E-filing: MNCIS / MyMNCourts
- Confidentiality: Minn. R. Pub. Access 4 restricts child protection records

## Federal District Court (USDC)

### Caption

```
                  UNITED STATES DISTRICT COURT
                  [DISTRICT] DISTRICT OF [STATE]

[PLAINTIFF NAME],                    )
                                     )    Case No. [##-cv-#####]
            Plaintiff,               )
                                     )    [DOCUMENT TITLE]
       v.                            )
                                     )    Judge: [JUDGE]
[DEFENDANT NAMES],                   )    Magistrate: [MAGISTRATE]
                                     )
            Defendants.              )    JURY TRIAL DEMANDED
```

### Federal specifics

- FRCP governs civil procedure
- Local rules vary by district — read the district's Local Rules carefully
- E-filing: PACER / CM/ECF (attorneys); pro se litigants often file paper with copy on disk
- Pleadings: numbered paragraphs, single fact per paragraph
- Twombly/Iqbal pleading standard: factual content sufficient to "state a claim to relief that is plausible on its face"
- Verification required if seeking certain relief (TROs, etc.)
- IFP under 28 USC §1915

### Federal civil cover sheet (JS-44)

Required with every new complaint. Form available at uscourts.gov.

## Federal appellate (Circuit Court)

- FRAP governs
- Brief format per Circuit Rule (e.g., 7th Cir. R. 32, 8th Cir. R. 28A)
- Word count limits vary; current FRAP 32(a)(7): 13,000 words for principal brief
- Required: cover, table of contents, table of authorities, jurisdictional statement, statement of issues, statement of case, statement of facts, summary of argument, argument, conclusion, certificates (compliance, service, type-volume)
- Appendix or designated record

## State appellate courts

### Wisconsin Court of Appeals

- Wis. Stat. ch. 809 (Rules of Appellate Procedure)
- Brief: §809.19; cover §809.19(1); appendix §809.19(2)
- Type-volume certification or page limit
- Three-judge panels typical

### Minnesota Court of Appeals

- Minn. R. Civ. App. P.
- Brief: Rule 128
- Word count limit

## Certificate of Service (universal template)

```
                    CERTIFICATE OF SERVICE

I hereby certify that on [DATE], I caused the foregoing [DOCUMENT TITLE]
to be served upon the following persons by [METHOD]:

[NAME], [ROLE]
[ADDRESS or EMAIL]

[NAME], [ROLE]
[ADDRESS or EMAIL]

                                    _______________________________
                                    [YOUR NAME]
                                    [ADDRESS]
                                    [PHONE]
                                    [EMAIL]
                                    Pro Se / [or attorney info]
```

## Proposed order format

Always submit a proposed order with any motion. Title: "PROPOSED ORDER" or "[PROPOSED] ORDER." Format:

```
[CAPTION]

ORDER

THIS MATTER having come before the Court on [movant's] [motion type] filed on [date], and the Court having considered the motion, any opposition, the record, and arguments of the parties, the Court hereby

FINDS THAT:

1. [Finding 1, anchored to record evidence]
2. ...

AND ORDERS THAT:

1. [Order 1, specific, measurable]
2. ...

3. The next [hearing type] in this matter is set for [date].

Dated this _____ day of __________, 20___.

                                    BY THE COURT:

                                    _______________________________
                                    [JUDGE NAME]
                                    Circuit Court Judge / District Judge
```

## Filing day checklist

- [ ] Caption correct for jurisdiction
- [ ] Case number correct
- [ ] Title accurately reflects content
- [ ] Numbered paragraphs (if motion)
- [ ] Required findings drafted in proposed order
- [ ] Certificate of service complete
- [ ] Service list current and accurate
- [ ] All exhibits attached or referenced and authenticated
- [ ] Signature line dated
- [ ] Filing fee paid or fee waiver filed
- [ ] Required number of copies prepared
- [ ] Confidentiality stamps / sealing applied where required
- [ ] E-filing PDF format compliant (text-searchable, OCR'd, within file size limits)
- [ ] Calendar entry for response deadline / hearing
- [ ] Backup of filed copy preserved

## Caveat

This skill provides defaults. ALWAYS verify against current Local Rules, judges' standing orders, and clerk's office practices in your specific court. Pro se litigants benefit from calling the clerk's office and asking, "I'm filing a [type] in a [type of case] — what does your court require?" Clerks often help.
